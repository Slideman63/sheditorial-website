about me

I have spent my career in ELT, initially as a teacher and Director of Studies, and latterly in Editorial roles in ELT publishing. I have worked for 10 years in each of these fields, and I enjoy drawing on my experience in both when working on new materials, in order to make them as interesting, engaging and effective as possible, for students and teachers alike.

Although I have experience in all sectors, I specialise in Pre-Primary and Primary materials.

I offer the following services:

  • Content development and liaising with authors
  • Content- and copy-editing of both manuscripts and proofs
  • AW and photo briefing, feedback and selection
  • Audio script preparation, attendance at recording sessions, and feedback
  • Digital mapping, manuscripting and release checking

If you’d like to get in touch about an upcoming project, please do contact me.

my experience

Freelance experience:
(2016 - present)

Since becoming a freelancer, I have completed a wide variety of work across Pre-Primary, Primary, Secondary and Adult sectors. I have worked on a number of very successful projects, with publishers including OUP, CUP, Pearson and Macmillan. My work has included:

  • developing manuscripts and attending author feedback meetings
  • content- and copy-editing proofs
  • AW, photo and audio briefs, and selecting and providing feedback on images
  • mapping, manuscripting, and checking releases of digital materials
  • writing tests and other supplementary materials
  • leading and managing Editorial teams, including in-house and other freelance editors

In-house experience:
Macmillan Education
(2011 - 2016)

  • Commissioning Editor (2015 – 2016)
  • Senior Managing Editor (2014–2015)
  • Managing Editor (2011–2014)

My time at Macmillan gave me extensive experience of editorial and project management work, as well as a thorough understanding of other aspects of the publishing process. I worked on a wide variety of projects, both brand new titles and versions, in both British and American English, for Spanish and International Primary and Pre-Primary markets.

As a Managing Editor, my role was a very broad one, including responsibility for both Content Development and Project Management. As such, I developed a wide range of skills including developing manuscripts, content- and copy-editing proofs, managing budgets, contracting and managing freelance editors, and liaising with Design and other departments to manage schedules. I also manuscripted and checked releases of digital components.

As Commissioning Editor, I was heavily involved in developing the concept of the highly successful Wheels series (Pre-Primary), as well as liaising closely with the author throughout the manuscript development and proofing stages.

my ELT background

Before moving into publishing, I spent ten years as an ELT teacher, Team Leader and Director of Studies in Spain and the UK. I taught children from age 5 to 16, adults from levels A1 – C2, including Exam Preparation classes and 1:1.

OISE Oxford Executive Centre

Director of Studies (2007–2010)

OISE Oxford delivers bespoke, short term intensive courses to executive clients, combining tailored 1:1 classes and small-group lessons.

As Director of Studies, I had overall responsibility for the Academic side of the school. My role included assessing each client’s needs, matching them with the appropriate tutors, and creating an overall timetable each week which made sure that each client’s needs were fully met. I also recruited and supported teachers in delivering the bespoke classes that clients needed.

OISE Young Learners'
Summer Schools

Academic Co-Ordinator (2005–2007)

OISE Young Learners runs multiple summer schools across the UK for learners ages 7 – 17.

I wrote the five Lesson Plan Packs which formed the basis of all lessons taught across the centres. Each level (Junior, Pre-Intermediate, Intermediate, Upper Intermediate, Advanced) comprised three weeks of lessons covering Grammar, Reading/Listening, Vocabulary, Speaking, and Writing/Fluency.

I also recruited and gave induction training to around 150 teachers each summer across 14 centres, as well as visiting the centres and observing an supporting teachers as the courses were running.
